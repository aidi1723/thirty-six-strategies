# Integration Workflows

Use these examples as adaptation patterns. Do not copy them mechanically; fit the operators to the current incentive structure and constraints.

## Traffic and Marketing

Trigger: A competitor releases a strong low-price viral product or content campaign.

Useful operators:
- `围魏救赵`: Avoid direct price comparison; attack the hidden cost and risk dimension.
- `顺手牵羊`: Capture competitor search heat with adjacent educational, comparison, or diagnostic content.

Typical plan:
- Publish risk-check, hidden-cost, material-comparison, maintenance-cost, or warranty-verification content that intercepts the same search intent.
- Build a comparison matrix that makes the competitor's cheapness create doubt instead of desire.
- Add a low-friction lead magnet such as "低价采购风险清单" or "供应商验厂核查表".

## Supply Chain and Trade Negotiation

Trigger: A buyer pressures price by forwarding a much lower competitor quote.

Useful operators:
- `假痴不癫`: Do not immediately contest the quote; ask process and spec questions that expose non-equivalence.
- `上屋抽梯`: Offer a valuable technical step, then require clearer commitment conditions.

Typical plan:
- Reply calmly with questions about material grade, tolerance, packaging, warranty, delivery, certification, and payment terms.
- Provide a high-end drawing, sample plan, inspection checklist, or optimization suggestion.
- After the buyer invests attention, reframe the price around total risk, acceptance criteria, and a controlled concession.

## Contract Design

Trigger: Drafting an agent, distributor, integration, or framework cooperation agreement.

Useful operators:
- `暗度陈仓`: Put long-term governance rights inside ordinary operational clauses.
- `反客为主`: Provide the process, reporting, audit, and data structures that make your side the rule-setter.

Typical plan:
- Include routine reporting, review, compliance, data-sharing, renewal, and performance-scorecard clauses.
- Tie incentives to verified activity, customer data integrity, service levels, and milestone reviews.
- Avoid abusive or unenforceable clauses. Ask for jurisdiction-specific legal review before execution.

## News Interpretation

Trigger: News says a major player changed executives, open-sourced core technology, adjusted prices, or reorganized channels.

Useful operators:
- `抛砖引玉`: Treat the public move as a signal designed to draw ecosystem responses.
- `釜底抽薪`: Ask what dependency, cost base, standard, or supply route the move is trying to alter.

Typical plan:
- Separate surface narrative from likely strategic intent.
- Identify affected stakeholders: customers, developers, suppliers, regulators, investors, and rivals.
- Produce a defensive memo: what not to copy, what to watch, what to secure, and where to move first.

## Business Negotiation

Trigger: Stalemate, opaque decision chain, one-sided demands, or unclear counterpart incentives.

Useful operators:
- `打草惊蛇`: Test a small condition to reveal real constraints.
- `擒贼擒王`: Locate the real blocker or decision owner.
- `李代桃僵`: Trade low-cost concessions for high-value terms.

Typical plan:
- Ask for one small operational commitment, document, timeline, or meeting with the actual owner.
- Keep concessions conditional, reversible, and tied to a valuable term.
- Define the walk-away line before entering the next round.
